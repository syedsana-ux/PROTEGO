Build Your Safety Plan

A safety plan helps you think clearly and act quickly when you feel unsafe. Follow these steps to create your own plan.

Step 1: Recognize Warning Signs

Identify behaviors, situations, or words that signal danger. Knowing these signs helps you act early.

Step 2: Prepare Emergency Contacts

List trusted friends, family, counselors, or local authorities you can call. Save their numbers under neutral names.

Step 3: Plan an Escape Route

Identify the safest exits in your home. Avoid small rooms or places with weapons. Keep spare keys accessible.

Step 4: Pack an Emergency Bag

Include ID proofs, money, medications, clothes, important documents, and emergency phone numbers.

Step 5: Create a Code Word

Choose a secret word you can say or text to alert trusted people that you need help immediately.

Step 6: Protect Digital Safety

Clear browser history, use private browsing, change passwords regularly, and disable location sharing.

Step 7: Know Local Support Services

Find shelters, helplines, and legal support near you. Use the Resources page to discover nearby help.
